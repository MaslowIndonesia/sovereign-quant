Why it compounds

You don't compound the average. You compound the survivors.

Two forces decide a long-run rate, and both punish drawdown. This is the whole reason a shallower book, with a slightly higher rate, pulls away over time.

A loss needs an outsized gain just to get back to even, and the deeper it goes, the more violently convex that math becomes.

The volatility tax. Geometric growth is roughly the average return minus half the variance. Every point of volatility is a toll on compounding. The hardened book runs at 14% volatility, not 19%, so it hands back far less each year.
